Living and Non-Living Things

Strand 1 · Life Science

Learn how to identify living things and understand the characteristics that define life.

Living vs Non-Living Things
Discover what makes something a living thing and what makes it non-living. Think of examples around your own home after watching!

All living things share 7 key characteristics including movement, growth, reproduction, and nutrition. See how animals and plants both show these traits!

Plants and Photosynthesis

Strand 1 · Life Science

Discover how plants make their own food and the vital role they play in our ecosystem.

How Plants Make Food – Photosynthesis
Plants are incredible food factories! Watch how leaves use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce sugar through photosynthesis.
Parts of a Plant and Their Functions
Explore the roots, stem, leaves, and flowers of a plant and learn the special job that each part does to keep the plant alive and healthy.

Animals

Strand 1 · Life Science

Explore the incredible diversity of the animal kingdom — their habitats, diets, and adaptations.

Animal Classification
Scientists sort animals into groups. Learn how mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, and insects are classified by their unique features.
Animal Habitats and Adaptations
Why do polar bears have thick fur? Why do fish have fins? Discover how animals have adapted perfectly to survive in their natural habitats.

The Human Body

Strand 1 · Life Science

Explore the incredible systems that keep your body working every second of every day.

The Circulatory System – Your Heart and Blood
Your heart beats about 100,000 times every day! Learn how the circulatory system pumps blood carrying oxygen and nutrients around your entire body.
The Human Skeleton and Muscles
Your skeleton has 206 bones that support and protect your body. Find out how bones and muscles work together to help you move, jump, and run!

The Environment

Strand 3 · Earth Science

Understand ecosystems, food chains, and our responsibility to protect the natural world.

Food Chains and Ecosystems
Energy passes from the sun to plants to animals in a food chain. See how all living things in an ecosystem depend on each other to survive.
Water, the Environment and the Water Cycle
Water is precious! Learn how the water cycle works and why it is so important to keep our rivers, lakes, and oceans clean for all living things.

The Solar System

Strand 3 · Earth & Space Science

Explore our solar system — the Sun, 8 planets, the Moon, and the wonders of outer space.

Exploring Our Solar System: Planets and Space
Take a tour of our Solar System! Learn about all 8 planets — from tiny Mercury closest to the Sun, to giant Jupiter, all the way to distant Neptune.
Earth's Rotation, Revolution and Day and Night
Why do we have day and night? Learn how the Earth rotates on its axis every 24 hours, and orbits the Sun every 365 days to give us our seasons.

States of Matter

Strand 2 · Physical Science

Everything around us is made of matter. Discover solids, liquids, gases, and how they change.

3 States of Matter: Solid, Liquid and Gas
Learn all about the three states of matter — solid, liquid, and gas. Discover how particles behave differently in each state and how matter changes form.
The Water Cycle: Evaporation and Condensation
Water changes state constantly on Earth! See how evaporation and condensation power the water cycle — and how you can make your own mini water cycle.

Weather and Seasons

Strand 3 · Earth Science

What causes rain? How are weather and climate different? Explore weather patterns and Ghana's two seasons.

Weather vs. Climate — What is the Difference?
Weather and climate are not the same thing. Weather changes daily, while climate describes long-term patterns. Learn the difference and why it matters.

Find out how moving air, wind patterns, and ocean currents create the weather we experience every day — and why weather can be so difficult to predict.
