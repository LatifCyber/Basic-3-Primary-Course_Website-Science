Strand 1 · Life Science

🌿 Living and Non-Living Things

All around us, there are many different things. Some things are alive and others are not alive. Scientists call them living things and non-living things.

🟢 What are Living Things?

Living things are things that are alive. All living things share certain features — we call these characteristics of living things:

🌱
Growth
They grow bigger over time
🌬️
Breathing
They take in and release gases
🍎
Nutrition
They need food for energy
🏃
Movement
They can move or respond
🐣
Reproduction
They can produce young ones
💡
Response
They react to their surroundings

Examples of living things: humans, animals, plants, fungi, and bacteria.

⚪ What are Non-Living Things?

Non-living things do not grow, breathe, eat, reproduce, or respond to the world around them. Examples include: rocks 🪨, water 💧, chairs 🪑, cars 🚗, pencils ✏️, and sand.

📝 Remember: Ask yourself — does it grow? Does it breathe? Can it reproduce? If YES to most — it is a living thing! If NO to all — it is a non-living thing.

⚡ Tricky Cases!

Fire grows and uses oxygen — but it is NOT living because it cannot reproduce or respond to things around it. A car moves but is NOT living because it needs a human to drive it and cannot grow or reproduce.

🌳🪨 Living vs Non-Living
💡

Did you know? There are millions of tiny living things called microorganisms that are so small you cannot see them without a microscope — yet they are very much alive! 🔬

🌱 Plants and Photosynthesis

Plants are very special living things — they are the only living things that can make their own food using sunlight, water and air. This makes them extremely important for all life on Earth.

🌿 Parts of a Plant and Their Jobs

🌿
Roots
Absorb water & minerals from soil; anchor the plant
🪵
Stem
Carries water up to leaves; supports the plant
🍃
Leaves
Make food using sunlight (photosynthesis)
🌸
Flower
Helps the plant reproduce by making seeds
🍎
Fruit
Protects seeds; provides food for animals
🌰
Seeds
Grow into new plants (reproduction)

☀️ What is Photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants make their own food. It happens mainly in the leaves.

☀️ Sunlight + 💧 Water + 💨 Carbon Dioxide  →  🍬 Sugar (food) + 🌬️ Oxygen

The green colour in leaves comes from a substance called chlorophyll. Chlorophyll captures sunlight and uses its energy to make food. Tiny pores on the underside of leaves called stomata allow carbon dioxide to enter and oxygen to escape.

💧 What Do Plants Need to Survive?

☀️ Sunlight — for energy to make food.
💧 Water — absorbed through roots from the soil.
🌬️ Carbon dioxide — taken in through stomata.
🌱 Good soil — provides minerals and nutrients.
🌡️ Warmth — most plants grow best in warm conditions.

🌍 Why are Plants Important?

Plants produce the oxygen we breathe, provide food for animals and humans, give shelter to animals, and help prevent soil erosion. We must protect our plants and forests!

🌱☀️ Photosynthesis
🌟

Did you know? One large tree produces enough oxygen for two people to breathe for an entire day! Planting trees is one of the best things we can do for our planet. 🌳🌳

🐾 Animals and Classification

Animals are living things that can move, eat, breathe, and reproduce. Scientists group animals into different classes to help us study them more easily. This grouping is called classification.

🍽️ Classification by Diet

🌿
Herbivores
Eat only plants
Cow, rabbit, elephant
🍖
Carnivores
Eat only animals
Lion, eagle, shark
🍽️
Omnivores
Eat plants & animals
Human, pig, bear

🔬 Classification by Body Features

🦴 Vertebrates — animals with a backbone.
   • Mammals: warm-blooded, have hair/fur, feed young with milk. Examples: humans, dogs, elephants, bats, dolphins.
   • Birds: have feathers and wings, most can fly, lay eggs. Examples: eagle, parrot, hen.
   • Reptiles: cold-blooded, covered in scales. Examples: lizard, crocodile, snake, tortoise.
   • Amphibians: live on land and in water, breathe through gills when young. Examples: frogs, toads, salamanders.
   • Fish: live in water, breathe through gills, covered in scales. Examples: tilapia, catfish, shark.

🐛 Invertebrates — animals WITHOUT a backbone. Examples: insects, spiders, snails, worms.

🐛 Insects — A Special Group

Insects are invertebrates with exactly 6 legs and 3 body parts (head, thorax, abdomen). Examples: ants, bees, butterflies, grasshoppers.
⚠️ Spiders are NOT insects — they have 8 legs and are called arachnids.

🐸 Life Cycles

Animals go through stages of life called a life cycle.
🦋 Butterfly: Egg → Caterpillar → Pupa (cocoon) → Butterfly.
🐸 Frog: Egg → Tadpole → Young Frog → Adult Frog.

🏠 Animal Habitats

The place where an animal naturally lives is called its habitat. Fish live in water, lions live on grasslands (savanna), monkeys live in forests, and camels live in deserts.

🦁🐟🦋 Animal Groups
🐝

Did you know? Bees help plants reproduce by carrying pollen from flower to flower — this is called pollination. Without bees, many fruits we eat would not exist! 🌸🍎

❤️ The Human Body

Your body is made up of many organs — special parts that each have a job to do. These organs work together in systems to keep you alive and healthy.

❤️ The Circulatory System

The heart is a muscular organ about the size of your fist. It pumps blood through blood vessels to every part of your body. Blood carries oxygen and nutrients to your cells and takes carbon dioxide away. Your heart beats about 100,000 times every day!

🫁 The Respiratory System

You breathe using your lungs — you have two of them in your chest. When you breathe in, your lungs fill with oxygen. When you breathe out, they release carbon dioxide. The lungs and heart work together to supply oxygen to the whole body.

🧠 The Nervous System

The brain is the control centre of your entire body. It sends messages through nerves to control your movement, senses, thoughts, and feelings. The brain, spinal cord, and nerves form the nervous system.

🦴 The Skeletal System

Your skeleton is made up of 206 bones. It gives your body its shape, supports you when you stand, and protects your soft organs (e.g. your skull protects your brain; your ribcage protects your heart and lungs). Muscles are attached to bones and help you move.

🍎 The Digestive System

When you eat, the digestive system breaks food down into tiny nutrients your body can absorb and use for energy. The main organs are the mouth, stomach, and intestines.

👀 The Five Senses

👁️
Sight
Eyes — see things
👂
Hearing
Ears — hear sounds
👃
Smell
Nose — detect smells
👅

✋
Touch
Skin — feel things

📝 Keep your body healthy! Eat balanced meals 🥗, drink clean water 💧, exercise regularly 🏃, wash your hands 🧼, and get enough sleep 😴.

❤️🧠🦴 Body Organs
💪

Did you know? Babies are born with about 270 bones, but as they grow some bones fuse together, so adults end up with only 206 bones! 🦴

🌍 Our Environment and Conservation

The environment is everything around us — the air, water, soil, plants, and animals. We all depend on a healthy environment to survive. Protecting it is everyone's responsibility, including yours!

⚠️ What Harms Our Environment?

🔥 Burning rubbish — pollutes the air with harmful smoke.
🪓 Deforestation — cutting too many trees destroys habitats and causes flooding and drought.
🏭 Industrial waste — factories releasing chemicals into rivers and the air causes water and air pollution.
🗑️ Littering — throwing rubbish on the ground or into water bodies harms animals and people.
🛢️ Burning fossil fuels — releasing carbon dioxide contributes to global warming and climate change.

🌐 The Ozone Layer

The ozone layer is a protective shield high up in the atmosphere that blocks the Sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays from reaching us. Certain chemicals from aerosol sprays and factories are damaging it, which is why we must reduce pollution.

♻️ How Can We Help? — Conservation

Conservation means protecting and carefully using our natural resources so they are available for future generations. Here is what YOU can do:

♻️
Recycle
Turn old materials into new products
🌳
Plant Trees
Restore forests and provide oxygen
💧
Save Water
Turn off taps; fix leaks
🚶
Walk/Cycle
Reduce car fumes and pollution
🗑️
No Littering
Always use a bin
☀️
Solar Energy
Use clean, renewable energy

⚡ Renewable vs Non-Renewable Energy

Renewable energy will never run out: ☀️ solar, 💨 wind, 🌊 hydroelectric.
Non-renewable energy will one day run out: 🛢️ petroleum, ⛏️ coal, gas. Ghana is investing in solar energy to power a cleaner future!

🌳♻️💧 Clean Environment
🌊

Did you know? A plastic bag can take more than 500 years to break down in the environment! Always use reusable bags and dispose of waste responsibly. 🛍️

☀️ The Solar System

Our Solar System is made up of the Sun and everything that orbits (moves around) it — including 8 planets, their moons, comets, and asteroids.

🌞 The Sun

The Sun is a massive ball of hot burning gas at the centre of our Solar System. It is our closest star and gives us light and heat. Without the Sun, there would be no life on Earth. The Sun is so big that over one million Earths could fit inside it!

🪐 The Eight Planets (in order from the Sun)

⚫
1. Mercury
Closest to the Sun; smallest planet
🟡
2. Venus
Hottest planet; brightest in our sky
🌍
3. Earth
Our home; has water and life
🔴
4. Mars
The Red Planet; has the tallest volcano
🟠
5. Jupiter
Largest planet in the Solar System
🪐
6. Saturn
Famous for its beautiful rings
🔵
7. Uranus
Spins on its side; very cold
🌑
8. Neptune
Farthest planet; very windy

🌕 The Moon

The Moon is Earth's natural satellite. It orbits (goes around) the Earth approximately once every 28 days. The Moon does not produce its own light — it reflects light from the Sun.

🌓 Day and Night

The Earth rotates (spins) on its axis once every 24 hours. The side facing the Sun has day and the side facing away from the Sun has night. This is why Ghana has daytime while another country on the other side of the Earth has night!

🗓️ The Year

The Earth orbits (travels around) the Sun once in 365 days — this is one year.

☀️🪐🌍 Solar System
🚀

Did you know? Light from the Sun travels 150 million kilometres to reach Earth — yet it arrives in just about 8 minutes! Light is the fastest thing in the universe. 💫

Strand 2 · Physical Science

🧪 States of Matter

Everything around us is made of matter. Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. Matter exists in three main states: solid, liquid, and gas.

🧊 Solids

A solid has a fixed shape and a fixed volume (size). Its particles are packed closely together in a regular pattern. Examples: rock, wood, ice, chair, bread.

💧 Liquids

A liquid has a fixed volume but NO fixed shape — it takes the shape of its container. Its particles can flow past each other. Examples: water, juice, oil, blood, mercury.

🌬️ Gases

A gas has NO fixed shape and NO fixed volume. Its particles spread out to fill any container completely. Examples: air, oxygen, carbon dioxide, steam.

🔄 Changes of State

🧊→💧
Melting
Solid → Liquid (when heated)
💧→🌬️
Evaporation
Liquid → Gas (when heated)
💧→🧊
Freezing
Liquid → Solid (when cooled)
🌬️→💧
Condensation
Gas → Liquid (when cooled)

🌡️ Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C. Below 0°C, water is ice (solid). Between 0°C and 100°C, it is liquid water. Above 100°C, it becomes steam (gas).

🌊 The Water Cycle

Water on Earth moves in a cycle that uses changes of state: Water evaporates from oceans and rivers → rises and cools to form clouds (condensation) → falls as rain or hail (precipitation) → flows back to rivers and oceans and the cycle begins again!

🧊💧🌬️ Solid · Liquid · Gas
💡

Did you know? Water is the only common substance on Earth that naturally exists in all three states — as ice ❄️, liquid water 💧, and water vapour (steam) ♨️ — all at the same time in nature!

🌦️ Weather and Seasons

Weather is the condition of the atmosphere at a particular place and time. It describes how hot or cold, wet or dry, windy or calm the air is. Weather can change from day to day.

🌡️ Weather Conditions

☀️ Sunny — clear skies, bright sunlight, hot.
🌧️ Rainy — water falls from clouds as rain.
☁️ Cloudy — sky covered by clouds, less sunlight.
🌬️ Windy — air moving quickly from one place to another.
❄️ Cold / Cool — low temperature; common in the dry season.

🌡️ Weather Instruments

🌡️
Thermometer
Measures temperature (hot or cold)
🌀
Anemometer
Measures wind speed
☂️
Rain Gauge
Measures how much rain has fallen
🧭
Wind Vane
Shows the direction of wind

📺 What is a Weather Forecast?

A weather forecast is a prediction of what the weather will be like in the coming hours or days. Scientists called meteorologists study the atmosphere to give us these forecasts. In Ghana, the Ghana Meteorological Agency (GMet) provides weather forecasts.

🗓️ Seasons in Ghana

Ghana is near the equator, so it experiences two main seasons instead of four:

🌧️
Rainy Season
Heavy rainfall; good for farming. Southern Ghana has two rainy seasons.
☀️
Dry Season
Little or no rain; sometimes cold at night. The Harmattan wind blows.

🌍 Countries further from the equator experience four seasons: Spring 🌸, Summer ☀️, Autumn 🍂, and Winter ❄️.

💨 What Causes Wind?

Wind is caused by air moving from areas of high pressure to areas of low pressure. The Sun heats the Earth unevenly, causing some air to rise and cooler air to rush in — this rushing air is wind!

🌧️ What Causes Rainfall?

The Sun heats water in oceans and rivers, causing evaporation. Water vapour rises, cools, and forms clouds (condensation). When clouds collect enough water droplets, they fall as rain. This is part of the water cycle.

🌡️🌧️☀️ Weather & Seasons
🌈

Did you know? A rainbow forms when sunlight passes through tiny water droplets in the air after rain. The droplets act like prisms and split the white light into 7 colours: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet! 🌈
